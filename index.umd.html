About

Mechanism-first biofilm science, with a focus on accessibility and mentorship.

I study how bacterial exopolysaccharides are synthesized, regulated, and remodeled before export, and how those molecular states shape extracellular matrix properties and emergent community behavior. My current focal target is NfrB, a membrane-embedded glycosyltransferase regulated by cyclic di-GMP, along with pathway remodeling enzymes such as YbcH.

Alongside research, I care about widening access to scientific pathways and tools. I serve as Program Lead for the La Familia Peer Mentoring Program at UMD and as an instructor and mentor with InspiraSTEM (El Salvador), where I develop practical computational biochemistry training that can run in-browser using open tools.

Research

Core areas and current projects (methods-forward, reproducible, and mechanism-driven).

Nfr System and Biofilm Exopolysaccharides

Mechanistic characterization of Nfr-mediated ManNAc polymer synthesis, regulation, and remodeling in E. coli biofilms, and how these states map to measurable matrix architecture.

EPS pathways cyclic di-GMP biofilm phenotype

Membrane Protein Biochemistry and cryo-EM

Detergent-free isolation and reconstitution of membrane enzymes into synthetic nanodiscs via copolymer screening, supporting single-particle cryo-EM grid preparation and iterative optimization for structure determination.

nanodiscs copolymers single-particle cryo-EM

Model-Informed Computation and Open Workflows

Docking and molecular dynamics to evaluate polymer fragment binding modes, extract interaction fingerprints, and nominate candidate residues for targeted mutagenesis. I also contribute to open-source, notebook-first workflows for accessible modeling and docking.

docking molecular dynamics reproducible notebooks
