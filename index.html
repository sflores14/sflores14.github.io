About

Mechanism-first biofilm science, with a focus on accessibility and mentorship.

I study how bacterial exopolysaccharides are synthesized, regulated, and remodeled before export, and how those molecular states shape extracellular matrix properties and community behavior. My current focus is the Nfr pathway in E. coli, including the membrane-embedded glycosyltransferase NfrB and associated remodeling enzymes.

Alongside research, I care about widening access to scientific tools and pathways. I support academic mentoring and science-policy engagement through student-led programs and training efforts that emphasize reproducible, notebook-first workflows.

Research

Focused themes and ongoing projects.

Biofilm Exopolysaccharide Pathways

Mechanistic characterization of the Nfr system and ManNAc polymer production, including regulation by cyclic di-GMP and connections to measurable biofilm phenotypes.

EPS pathways cyclic di-GMP biofilm phenotype

Membrane Protein Biochemistry and cryo-EM

Detergent-free extraction and reconstitution of membrane enzymes into nanodiscs via copolymer screening to support single-particle cryo-EM and structure-guided experiments.

nanodiscs copolymers single-particle cryo-EM

Computation and Reproducible Workflows

Docking and molecular dynamics to test polymer fragment interactions, extract interaction fingerprints, and nominate residues for mutagenesis—packaged into transparent, reusable notebooks.
