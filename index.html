Bio

I am an assistant professor in the School of Information, where I direct the Civic AI Lab. My work blends qualitative fieldwork with machine learning to understand how communities adopt and reshape technology. I collaborate with public agencies, community partners, and students to co-design tools that are transparent, accountable, and accessible.

Previously, I was a postdoctoral fellow at the Center for Data & Society. I earned my Ph.D. in Informatics from the University of Michigan and my B.S. in Computer Engineering from the University of Texas at El Paso.

Research Interests

Human-centered AI Civic technology Algorithmic accountability Data justice Participatory design Public interest ML

My research asks how AI systems can be designed with community values in mind. I study interpretability and governance approaches that keep decision-making in the hands of those most affected by automated tools.
